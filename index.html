What does "Slay" mean?

Gen Z Performance

Definition: To do something exceptionally well or to look exceptionally good.

Example: "Did you see her performance? She totally slayed!"

Detailed Explanation

The term "slay" has evolved from its original meaning of killing someone to a positive expression of excellence. In modern slang, it's used to compliment someone's outstanding performance, appearance, or achievements.

Origin

Originally from drag culture and African American Vernacular English (AAVE), the term gained mainstream popularity through social media platforms like Instagram and TikTok.

Usage Tips

  • Use "slay" to compliment someone's outfit: "That dress is slaying!"
  • Can be used as a verb: "She slayed that presentation!"
  • Often accompanied by fire emojis (🔥) for emphasis

Understanding "No Cap"

AAVE Truth

Definition: No lie or for real.

Example: "I aced that test, no cap!"

Detailed Explanation

"No cap" is used to emphasize that someone is telling the truth or being completely honest. It's often used to add credibility to a statement that might otherwise seem exaggerated.

Origin

Originated from African American Vernacular English (AAVE), where "cap" means to lie or exaggerate. The phrase became popular through rap music and then spread to mainstream youth culture.

Usage Tips

  • Use at the end of a statement to emphasize truthfulness
  • Often used with achievements or surprising facts
  • Can be shortened to just "no cap" in response to someone else's statement

The "Italian Animals" Meme Explained

Meme Viral

What is it? A viral meme featuring photoshopped images of Italian landmarks with random animals added in absurd ways.

Detailed Explanation

The Italian Animals meme involves placing random animals in famous Italian settings in ways that are completely absurd but visually humorous. The contrast between the majestic Italian architecture and the random animals creates the comedic effect.

Origin

Began on Twitter in early 2023, possibly inspired by similar absurdist meme trends. Gained massive popularity on TikTok with users creating their own versions.

Popular Examples

  • A giraffe leaning against the Leaning Tower of Pisa
  • A kangaroo in a gondola in Venice
  • A panda sitting at a café in Rome
  • A hippo in the Trevi Fountain

Why It's Funny

The humor comes from the complete randomness and the animals' nonchalant expressions in these iconic settings, as if they belong there.

What's "Rizz"?

Charisma Dating

Definition: Short for charisma, refers to someone's ability to attract romantic partners.

Example: "He's got so much rizz, he can get anyone's number."

Detailed Explanation

"Rizz" describes someone's natural charm and ability to attract romantic or sexual partners through charisma, confidence, and smooth talking.

Origin

Popularized by Twitch streamer Kai Cenat in 2022. The term is a shortened form of "charisma" (pronounced "riz-zuh-ma" in some dialects).

Usage Tips

  • Can be used as a noun: "He's got rizz"
  • Can describe actions: "He's rizzing her up"
  • Often used in dating contexts but can apply to general charisma

"Glow Up" Meaning

Transformation Appearance

Definition: A significant transformation in appearance, usually for the better.

Example: "Did you see Mark's glow up after high school?"

Detailed Explanation

A "glow up" refers to a positive transformation in someone's appearance, style, or overall presentation. It often implies that someone has become more attractive or put-together compared to their past self.

Origin

Originated in Black culture to describe both physical and personal growth. Gained mainstream popularity through social media transformation posts.

Usage Tips

  • Often used with before/after photos
  • Can refer to style, confidence, or success transformations
  • Sometimes used ironically for minor improvements

What does "Mid" mean?

Quality Rating

Definition: Mediocre, average, not special.

Example: "That new movie was so mid, I fell asleep."

Detailed Explanation

"Mid" is used to describe something that's average or mediocre - not good enough to be impressive, but not bad enough to be memorable. It's a dismissive term for things that fail to stand out.

Origin

Comes from the middle of a ranking scale (not high or low). Popularized in hip-hop culture before spreading to mainstream youth slang.

Usage Tips

  • Often used to critique music, movies, or fashion
  • Can be extended to "mid-tier" for slightly better than mid
  • Sometimes used as a burn: "Your taste is mid"

"Based" Explained

Opinion Approval

Definition: Expressing approval for someone's bold opinion or stance.

Example: "You think pineapple belongs on pizza? Based."

Detailed Explanation

"Based" is used to show approval for someone's opinion, especially when it's controversial or goes against mainstream thinking. It implies that the person is being authentic and true to themselves.

Origin

Originally from rapper Lil B ("The BasedGod"), who used it to describe being true to oneself. Evolved to its current meaning through internet culture.

Usage Tips

  • Often used in response to unpopular but honest opinions
  • Can be paired with "and redpilled" for extra emphasis
  • Sometimes used ironically for obviously popular opinions

"Touch Grass" Meaning

Internet Advice

Definition: Telling someone to go outside and experience real life.

Example: "You've been online for 10 hours straight. Go touch grass."

Detailed Explanation

"Touch grass" is a suggestion (often sarcastic) that someone needs to disconnect from the internet and engage with the real world. It implies that someone's online behavior is disconnected from reality.

Origin

Began as internet slang in online gaming communities, later spread to broader internet culture as a way to call out terminally online behavior.

Usage Tips

  • Used when someone is overly invested in online arguments
  • Can be a genuine suggestion or an insult
  • Often accompanied by "go outside" for extra emphasis

"Sus" - More Than Among Us

Suspicious Gaming

Definition: Suspicious or shady behavior.

Example: "Why is Jake being so quiet? That's sus."

Detailed Explanation

"Sus" is shorthand for suspicious, used to describe behavior that seems shady, dishonest, or out of the ordinary. While popularized by Among Us, it predates the game in African American Vernacular English.

Origin

Existed in AAVE for years but exploded in popularity through the game Among Us in 2020, where players try to identify "sus" (suspicious) impostors.

Usage Tips

  • Can be used as an adjective: "That's sus"
  • Or as a noun: "He's acting like a sus"
  • Often accompanied by side-eye emoji (🤨) for extra effect

"Cheugy" - The New Cringe

Style Trends

Definition: Something that's trying too hard to be trendy but is actually outdated.

Example: "Live laugh love signs are so cheugy."

Detailed Explanation

"Cheugy" describes aesthetic choices that are trying to be trendy but are actually basic, outdated, or trying too hard. It's often used to describe millennial trends that Gen Z finds cringeworthy.

Origin

Coined in 2013 by a Beverly Hills high school student but went viral on TikTok in 2021 as Gen Z began mocking millennial fashion and decor trends.

Common Cheugy Things

  • "Live, Laugh, Love" decor
  • Ugg boots with skinny jeans
  • Starbucks pumpkin spice lattes
  • Minion memes
  • Anything "basic" from the early 2010s

"Stan" Culture

Fandom Music

Definition: An extremely or excessively enthusiastic fan.

Origin: From Eminem's song "Stan" about an obsessed fan.

Detailed Explanation

A "stan" is an overzealous fan who is extremely dedicated to a celebrity or franchise. While originally negative, it's now often used neutrally or positively to describe dedicated fans.

Origin

From Eminem's 2000 song "Stan" about an obsessive fan who kills himself and his pregnant girlfriend after Eminem doesn't reply to his letters.

Modern Usage

  • Can be used as a noun: "She's a BTS stan"
  • Or as a verb: "I stan this artist"
  • Stan culture refers to the behaviors of these fan communities
  • Often involves intense social media support and defense of their idol

"Simp" - Not Just a Name

Relationships Internet

Definition: Someone who does too much for someone they're attracted to.

Example: "He bought her a $100 gift after one date? Total simp."

Detailed Explanation

A "simp" is someone (usually a man) who goes above and beyond for someone they're attracted to, often without receiving the same energy in return. It implies they're being overly submissive or desperate for attention.

Origin

Originally short for "simpleton," the term was used in hip-hop culture before being popularized on Twitch and TikTok in 2020.

Usage Tips

  • Often used in dating contexts but can apply to any unbalanced relationship
  • Can be used as a noun or verb: "Stop simping for her"
  • Sometimes used ironically among friends

"Yeet" - Throw or Excitement?

Action Versatile

Definition: To throw something with force OR an exclamation of excitement.

Example: "Yeet that trash!" or "We won the game! Yeet!"

Detailed Explanation

"Yeet" is a versatile term that can mean to throw something with force or serve as an exclamation of excitement or victory. The meaning depends on context.

Origin

Originated in the early 2000s in dance culture (specifically the "yeet dance"), then evolved into its current meanings through Vine and later TikTok.

Usage Tips

  • As a verb: "Yeet the ball!" (throw it hard)
  • As an exclamation: "Yeet! I passed the test!"
  • The past tense is "yeeted" or "yote" (jokingly)
  • Can be used when discarding something: "Yeet it in the trash"

"Bussin'" - Not Just Buses

Food Quality

Definition: Really good, especially referring to food.

Example: "This pizza is bussin'!"

Detailed Explanation

"Bussin'" describes something that's extremely good, particularly food. It implies that something is so delicious it's worth getting excited about.

Origin

From African American Vernacular English (AAVE), possibly related to "busting" with flavor. Gained popularity through food TikTok and rap lyrics.

Usage Tips

  • Most commonly used for food but can apply to other things
  • Often said while eating: *takes bite* "This is bussin'!"
  • Can be extended: "Bussin' bussin'" for extra emphasis

"FOMO" - Fear Of Missing Out

Psychology Social

Definition: Anxiety that an exciting event may be happening elsewhere.

Example: "I wasn't going to go, but I got FOMO."

Detailed Explanation

FOMO describes the anxiety that others might be having rewarding experiences from which you're absent. It's often triggered by seeing social media posts about events you weren't invited to or chose not to attend.

Origin

Coined in 2004 by marketing strategist Dan Herman, but popularized in the 2010s with the rise of social media platforms that constantly show others' activities.

Usage Tips

  • Often used as a reason for attending events: "I only came because of FOMO"
  • Can describe the feeling: "I have such FOMO right now"
  • The opposite is JOMO (Joy Of Missing Out)

"GOAT" - Greatest Of All Time

Praise Sports

Definition: The best in a particular field or category.

Example: "Messi is the GOAT of soccer."

Detailed Explanation

GOAT is used to describe someone who is considered the best ever in their field. It's the highest compliment you can give in competitive contexts.

Origin

Popularized by boxer Muhammad Ali who called himself "the greatest." The acronym was cemented in sports culture and later spread to other domains.

Usage Tips

  • Can be used as a noun: "She's the GOAT"
  • Or as an adjective: "That was a GOAT performance"
  • Often accompanied by goat emoji (🐐)
  • Common in sports but applies to any field (music, acting, etc.)
